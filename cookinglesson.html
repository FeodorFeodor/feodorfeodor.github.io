Introduction to Cooking

Cooking is the art of preparing food by using heat. It is a process that has been around for centuries, and it is one of the most important skills that anyone can learn. Cooking can be a fun and rewarding hobby, and it can also be a way to save money and eat healthier.

There are many different cooking techniques, and each one has its own unique flavor and texture. Some of the most common cooking techniques include:

  • Boiling: This is a simple cooking method that involves submerging food in boiling water. Boiling is a good way to cook vegetables, pasta, and eggs.
  • Frying: This is a cooking method that involves cooking food in hot oil. Frying can be used to cook a variety of foods, including meats, vegetables, and pastries.
  • Roasting: This is a cooking method that involves cooking food in an oven. Roasting is a good way to cook meats, vegetables, and whole chickens.
  • Sautéing: This is a cooking method that involves cooking food in a pan over medium heat. Sautéing is a good way to cook vegetables, meats, and seafood.
  • Baking: This is a cooking method that involves cooking food in an oven. Baking is a good way to cook cakes, breads, and pastries.
In addition to these basic cooking techniques, there are also a number of more advanced techniques, such as braising, grilling, and steaming. With a little practice, you can learn to master these techniques and create delicious and impressive dishes.

No matter what your skill level, there are a few basic principles that every cook should know.

These principles include:

  • Use fresh ingredients: Fresh ingredients always taste better than frozen or canned ingredients.
  • Season your food: Seasoning is essential for bringing out the flavor of food.
  • Don't overcook your food: Overcooked food is dry and tasteless.
  • Have fun: Cooking should be enjoyable!
Here are some additional tips for beginners:
  • Start with simple recipes. There is no need to try to make a complicated dish when you are just starting out.
  • Read the recipe carefully before you start cooking. This will help you understand the steps involved and make sure that you have all of the ingredients.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment. Cooking is a creative process, so don't be afraid to try new things.

Learning Point 1: Boiling Water

Ingredients:

  • 1 pot
  • 1 lid
  • Fresh cold tap water
Instructions:
  1. Choose a pot with a greater volume than the amount of water you plan to boil.
  2. Pour fresh cold tap water into the pot. Make sure there's at least 3 inches of space between the rim of the pot and the surface of the water.
  3. Turn the burner to high place the pot on the burner.
  4. Cover the pot with a lid that fits snugly. This keeps in the heat and helps the water boil faster.
  5. They say a watched pot never boils, but it actually does. Just be patient and let the water come to a boil.
  6. Once the water is boiling, let it boil for 1-3 minutes. This will kill any harmfol bacteria in the water.
  7. Remove the pot from the heat and let the water cool slightly before using.

Learning Point 2: Frying

Frying: This is a cooking method that involves cooking food in hot oil. Frying can be used to cook a variety of foods, including meats, vegetables, and pastries.
  • Patties
  • Egg
  • Breadcrumbs
  • Oil for frying
  • Salt and pepper to taste
Instructions:
  1. Heat some oil in a pan over medium heat.
  2. Dip the patties in beaten egg, then coat them in breadcrumbs.
  3. Fry the patties for 2-3 minutes per side, or until they are golden brown and cooked through.
  4. Drain the patties on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  5. Season the patties with salt and pepper to taste.
Tips:
  • Use a neutral-flavored oil, such as vegetable oil or canola oil.
  • Don't overcrowd the pan, or the temperature of the oil will drop and the patties will not cook evenly.
  • Flip the patties halfway through cooking.
  • Drain the patties on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  • Serve the patties hot with your favorite toppings.
